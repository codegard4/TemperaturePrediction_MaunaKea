Maunakea Temperature Prediction Example

**Example only--Up to date version privately held

Description

  • Project Purpose:
    • To predict the outside temperature at the telescope at opening time. Utilizes temperature prediction for setting inner dome temperatures close to the outside, minimizing airflow disturbance to instruments.
  • Deliverables:
    • Background information on Maunakeaâ€™s temperature trends and correlations between predictive variables and temperature.
    • A model for temperature at the summit of Maunakea.
    • Descriptive statistics for model accuracy.
    • Plots overlaying the model on top of daily temperature data (Example is this page).
  • Proposed Solutions:
    • Historical temperature data without a model.
    • Linear and polynomial models for temperature.
    • Weighted model of temperature using historical data.
    • Neural network/machine learning algorithm.
  • Chosen Solution:
    • Using a trapezoidal integration model.
  • Data Import & Cleaning:
    • Data stored in two CSV files with 11 columns each.
    • Columns include date, time, various temperature readings, humidity, barometric pressure, and dewpoint.
    • Cleaning involves removing erroneous data points, filtering temperature and pressure values, and converting units.
  • Research Questions:
    • Investigates the effects of changing barometric pressure on temperature.
    • Explores patterns in warming and cooling cycles, potential aggregation by month, and the impact of linear and polynomial models.
    • Examines the correlation between daytime high and nighttime low temperatures.
